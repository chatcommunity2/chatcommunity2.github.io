Bridging Instead of Replacing Online Coding Communities with AI through Community-Enriched Chatbot Designs

Junling Wang, Lahari Goswami, Gustavo Kreia Umbelino, Kiara Chau, Mrinmaya Sachan, April Wang
ETH Zurich
DOI Paper Code

Abstract

LLM-based chatbots such as ChatGPT are increasingly used to support coding tasks, but they typically provide isolated answers with little support for social learning or contextual grounding. In contrast, online coding communities like Kaggle foster learning through peer-generated content, discussion, and shared practices. As reliance on LLMs grows, however, participation in these communities risks being reduced, weakening their collaborative value.

We introduce Community-Enriched AI, a design paradigm that embeds social learning dynamics from online coding communities into LLM-based chatbots by surfacing user-generated content alongside social signals. We instantiate this paradigm in ChatCommunity, a retrieval-augmented chatbot built on Kaggle resources. Across two studies with data science learners (N=28; N=12), we show that Community-Enriched AI increases trust, encourages engagement with community content, and effectively supports learners in completing data science tasks. Our findings highlight how AI assistance can bridge—rather than replace— online coding communities.

How it works

ChatCommunity uses a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipeline over Kaggle notebooks. Notebook content is chunked and indexed with competition and social metadata, retrieved and ranked based on user-selected criteria (relevance, votes, or views), and then used to generate responses with an LLM. Retrieved sources are displayed alongside the answer with their associated community signals.
